I would like to share another couple of simulations which are related to the previous post on the sine-Gordon equation. This time the geometry is a Y-junction (in contrast to T-junction shown below). The initial conditions are the same. These computations show one more time that the *qualitative* behaviour of the solution does not depend on the *angle* between the branches (but it depends on their **width**).
