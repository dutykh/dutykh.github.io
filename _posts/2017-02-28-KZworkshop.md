From 20th to 25th of February 2017 I participated in a workshop organized by the [Faculty of Mechanics and Mathematics](http://www.kaznu.kz/en/334/page/%20Departments/Faculty_of_Mechanics_and_Mathematics_) of the [Al-Farabi Kazakh National University](http://www.kaznu.kz/en/). The goal of this workshop was mainly to discuss a new Bachelor degree Programme *Applied and Computational Mathematics*. The particularity of this Programme is that all courses are going to be taught in **English**. This educational programme is a part of the "**Kazakhstan 2050**" Strategy.

Among the participants (besides the *Author* of this post) we had the pleasure to count the following experts:

* [Sergey Cherny](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Sergey_Cherny), Institute of Computational Technologies, SB RAS, Russia
* [Alexander Gasnikov](http://ait.mtas.ru/en/about/personal/gasnikov.php), Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology, Russia
* [Sergey Kabanikhin](http://www.math.nsc.ru/LBRT/u2/kabanikhin.html), Institute of Computational Mathematics and Mathematical Geophysics, SB RAS, Russia
* [Julius Kaplunov](https://www.keele.ac.uk/scm/staff/professors/juliuskaplunov/), Keele University, UK
* [Matthias Meinke](https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Matthias_Meinke), RWTH Aachen, Germany
* [Lian-Ping Wang](http://research.me.udel.edu/~lwang/), University of Delaware

The conclusions and recommendations issued during this workshop are going to be published in workshop materials to make outcomes available for other Kazakh (and *not only* Kazakh) Universities.
