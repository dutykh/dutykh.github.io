The Lecture notes are already available. They will be probably updated in the nearest future to the latest version (including some minor corrections and perhaps some new material):

* **D. Dutykh**. [A brief introduction to pseudo-spectral methods: application to diffusion problems](https://cel.archives-ouvertes.fr/cel-01256472/), Lecture Notes, 2016

Otherwise, recently with Didier Clamond we submitted a new mauscript where we report the [multi-symplectic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multisymplectic_integrator) structure for the Green-Naghdi-type system describing long internal waves evolution. It seems that this system was derived for the first by E. Barthélémy (1989) in his PhD thesis done at the University of Grenoble. For simplicity, we adopt the rigid lid approximation, but the multi-symplectic structure can be generalized to the free surface case as well. The preprint is already freely available to download:

* D. Clamond, **D. Dutykh**. [Multi-symplectic structure of fully-nonlinear weakly-dispersive internal gravity waves](https://hal.archives-ouvertes.fr/hal-01296552/), Submitted, 2016
