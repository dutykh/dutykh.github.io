We continue to work on the dynamics of thin liquid films. In particular, two my colleagues from the [University of Savoie Mont Blanc](https://www.univ-smb.fr/) - [Marx Chhay](http://marx.chhay.free.fr/) and [Marguerite Gisclon](http://www.lama.univ-savoie.fr/~gisclon/) have recently taken into account thermal effects in the thin film dynamics. A numerical simulation of the coupled model is shown here (*please, don't hesitate to open the window on the full screen*):

<iframe width="420" height="315" src="//www.youtube.com/embed/BDX07P3MlUo" frameborder="0"></iframe>

The system evolution starts from a slightly perturbed free surface and a constant temperature distribution. Around the time instance t = 30 *s* it arrives to a permanent shape composed of a finite number of periodic travelling waves. The numerical scheme is based on the [Rusanov numerical flux](http://grid.engin.umich.edu/~gtoth/SWMF/GM/BATSRUS/Doc/HTML/DESIGN/node22.html) and central finite differences for capillary and other source terms. The boundary conditions are periodic.
